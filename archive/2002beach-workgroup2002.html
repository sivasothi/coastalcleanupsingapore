Meetings & Recces

Sun 4 Aug: 2pm-5pm - First briefing at Raffles Museum. Preliminary discussion about the operation.

Site recces
Sun 11 Aug: Timothy recces East Coast.

 

Process flow for the ICCS Beach

1. Advertise to regulars and selected schools/organisations only (Andrew Tay, Wong Yueat Tin).

2. School registers interest to participate, with estimated number of participants. See webpage <http://mangrove.nus.edu.sg/iccs> for status; will be updated later tonight.

3. Yueat Tin confirms participation.

4. We allocate sites; if regulars, return them to their 2001 location.

5. Site recces are being conducted by:

* Timothy Pwee (East Coast),
* Calvin Koh & Chia Kin Wee, JJC students (Changi beach and extension),
* Christopher Pang (Sembawang & Punggol, Sengkang),
* David Teo (Pasir Ris) -  have not asked him as yet,
* Ho Shu Fen, RSYC (west coast beach, west coast park)

6. Pauline Tan informs NParks manager of activity (except for NYP Geocouncil sites). Hold off cleaning for the day, until 11am.

7. We forward schools/organisations the ICC pack (this will be done next week, once powerpoint settled and Nature Watch collected).

* Guide for organisers (prepared by Chris & NYP Geocouncil, 2001, updated),
* Powerpoint presentation for beach organisers (Siva, Calvin, Kin Wee & JJC students, 2002),
* Data Card 2002, with modified submission details for use in Singapore (Airani, 2002),
* Worksheet for schools (prepared by Lina Tan & Tan Beng Chiak, RGS, 2002),
* 2001 data and reports,  local and ICC, 2002
* Nature Watch article, etc., 2002
* CD ROM with soft copies of all relevant documents (relevant items will be available on the web)..

8. Data Manager, Airani, contacts them about data submission. Note that there is a new data card. This year, we will offer submission by excel and by webpage (Suresh, Airani). Organisers can choose to make their submissions either way, as a soft introduction. The webpage will collate submissions into a database for the Data Manager to download the final day.  

9. Where there are a large number of students involved, Chris and I are willing to go down and give an assembly talk &endash; can be based on the power point we are preparing for distribution to beach participants.   

10. We will offer a Coordinator & Student leaders briefing, Sat 7 Sep: 9am &endash; 12pm at NUS Science Auditorium . This will be presented by Kate, Airani & Siva, based on the 2001 briefing - <http://mangrove.nus.edu.sg/iccs/iccsbriefing-2002.html> Where possible, I will want others to present. This session is primarily a primer for methodology and motivational. It is more important for student leaders to attend rather than teachers. We will have to gauge response as well. It will be on for mangrove but will have to see about beach participation. LT31 booking has just been confirmed.

11. NUS issues NUS-NSS press release (provisional). Straits Times and NewsRadio have expressed interest in coverage. Target this year &endash; there is a lot of trash, most of it is cigarette butts and plastic, and a large proportion appears to come from shoreline activities.

12. More for novice organisations, liasons can help with preparations and managing the safety and data submission on the day. Chris, Kin Wee and Clavin are planning to liase; we are awaiting responses from Yussof Ishak and Queensway Secondary. I may have other undergrads, after a briefing I will hold next week.  

13. Exercise conducted, and data submitted. Post-cleanup ops begin.
