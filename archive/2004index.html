Habitatnews - Preliminary report (13 Oct 2004)

More than 2,000 of us from more than 40 institutions turned out on our beaches and mangroves to categorise and clear more than 9 tonnes of trash on 18 Sep 2004.

More teams in mangroves this year led to a hefty 5.9 tonnes or 64% of the total weight of trash collected in Singapore. 1.1 tonnes were cleared from Loyang, 1. 6 tonnes from Chek Jawa and 3.2 tonnes from Kranji mangroves.

However, most (62%) of the 88,663 items collected were from beaches.

Volunteeers removed and categorised >88,000 items this year. Last year we had counted >74,000 items, and in 2002, we collected >66,000 items.

Cigarette butts, are once again ranked No. 1 with some 11,867 butts were collected. Almost all were from beaches as only 10 were found in mangroves!

Bags (11,367), Foodwrappers (11, 052), straws (8.521) and styrofoam bits (7,216) are the next most numerous items. We obviously have a problem with plastics!

Plastic accounts for 72% of all items collected, and this proportion is higher in mangroves (86%) then on beaches (62%).

In both mangrove and beaches, it is shoreline activities that are contributing the most trash (28-30%). We have a ways to go, folks!

The cleanup at Chek Jawa.
