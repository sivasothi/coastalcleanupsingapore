2002 Data Summary

ICCS 2002 Page

The data collected is useful to all of us in many ways. It helps us better understand the source(s) of the marine debris and how we can further help to save our oceans.

Last year, we found more than 20,500 pieces of plastic bags and food wrappers and they feature as the top 2 items collected. There were also more than 8,000 pieces of cigarette butts and 7,500 straws!

Where did all these rubbish come from? 70% of all the trash we collected came from shoreline and recreational activities - activities on land such as picnics, camping, walking by the beach or even littering into the drains.

There are many ways we can help reduce the pollution in our oceans. You can do your part!

The Ocean Conservancy returns a report from the data that we submitted every year, along with reports from other parts of the world. Click here if you would like to download a copy of the 2002 Singapore report.
